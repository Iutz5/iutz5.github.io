Banjo...as a Cosmogram?

What even is a cosmogram?

A cosmogram depicts a cosmology in a flat geometric form

A cosmology is the study of the cosmos, and the relation between humans and entities in other spaces

What does this mean in the context of the Kongo culture?

In the Kongo culture, this depicts the physical world from the spiritual world.

The cosmogram represents the journey of the human soul, the intersection between the living the and dea, and the cyclical nature of existance

What does this have to do with the banjo?

Africans incorporated elements of the Kongo cosmogram into their cultural expressions, particularly in music and dance, as a means of resistance and preservation

Through dance, music, and symbolic gestures, Africans conveyed messages and maintained connections to their heritage

Connections to ancestors

It was common in Kongo religion to make a physical safe-ground for their ancestors. This was mostly done in the form of pottery, particularly having spirits reside in jars and containers

The banjo was originally designed to hold as a resting place for spiritual ancestors in the physical world, allowing for celebration through music and dance with the members of the physical world and members of the spirtual world

So how did these traditions and cultural practices get brought into the Americas?

The Kongo's religious traditions were mostly conversed through stories and tales. The listeners to these stories got taken away from their families in the 18th century, when the Trans-Atlantic Slave Trade was at its peak. Slavery is the Americas was necessary at the time for large agricultural and economic success

Slaves living on the plantations

The conditions for the slaves was extremely harsh. This was mostly due to slaveowners fearing of being overthrown by their slaves, since most slaveowners had a large number of slaves per acre

This fear put restrictions onto the slaves, including no verbal communcation, which forced the slaves to have to communicate through dance and body movement; something that was largely practiced in the Kongo culture as means of celebration
